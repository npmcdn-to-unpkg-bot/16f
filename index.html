Frequently Asked Questions

What is DubHacks?

DubHacks is the largest student-run college hackathon in the Pacific Northwest. The first of its kind in the region, student developers and designers gather at the University of Washington Seattle campus to form teams and build projects with the goal of creating solutions to real world problems and learning new technologies.

This is the third-ever DubHacks event and will feature a special focus on social impact. Emphasizing the role technology, communication, and community have on the society we live in today. By doing so this hackathon encourages the learning of new technical skills while also connecting passion driven students across the Pacific Northwest.

Who can attend?

Undergraduate university students of all backgrounds are encouraged to apply to and attend DubHacks.

What about travel reimbursment?

Travel reimbursement will be provided for this even on a case by case basis.

How much does this even cost?

Absolutely free. We will provide you WiFi, meals, caffeine, swag, and the workspace.

What should I bring?

Please bring a laptop, charger, student ID and passion! Besides that, a toothbrush, sleeping bag/blanket, and change of clothes is recommended.

How big are the teams?

A team can be as big as 5 people. We'll help with pairing team members if you don't come with one.

What should I build?

Anything really. The project is up to you and your team. You have 24 hours to make anything, ranging from web apps, desktop apps, mobile apps, hardware hacks, or even traditional software.

Are we allowed to build on past projects?

A hackathon is an experience to build on something completely new within a set amount of time. With that in mind, you cannot work on past projects. Using your own APIs or third-party APIs is alright as long as the project is a fresh start.

Will you have hardware prototyping equipment?

Yes. Our partners at MLH will have limited stock of Oculus Rifts, Myo armbands, Leap Motions, Arduinos, 3D printers and more to loan out to hackers for the duration of the hackathon.

I have a different question. Who can I ask?

Reach out to us at via Twitter, Facebook, or send an email to info@dubhacks.co.
